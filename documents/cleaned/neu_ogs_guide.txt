Avoiding Scams
1. Utilize Northeastern’s Off Campus Database
mentioned in this pamphlet.
2. Stay informed about market rental prices and
exercise caution with listings that appear “too
good to be true” (e.g., unusually low rent).
3. Inspect the apartment in person or through live
video walk-through before making any payments
to ensure its existence and legitimacy of
the lessor.
4. Be cautious of landlords who claim to be abroad
and are unable to meet in person.
5. Refrain from sharing your financial information
with anyone.
6. Avoid using cash, MoneyGram, Venmo, or other
cash apps for payments.
Seek assistance from Off Campus Engagement and
Support for any housing concerns.
Key Terms
• Addendum: Additional provisions to the lease
document.
• Co-signer/Guarantor: Party responsible for
tenant’s debt in case of rent default.
• Landlord/Lessor: Property owner.
• Lease: Legally binding contract for renting
property.
• Property Manager: Oversees daily property
operations.
• Realtor/Broker: Agent for apartment rental
transactions.
• Security Deposit: Sum paid by tenants before
moving in, refundable within 30 days if no
damages caused.

Common Lease Terms
apartment for you
• Jointly and Severally: All parties have joint liability;
each party is responsible for obligations.
• Legal Tenancy: Only individuals named on the
lease as tenants or lessees are legally permitted to
reside on the premises.
• Right to Entry: Your landlord has the right to enter
the apartment after giving a reasonable notice for
any valid reason.
• Lease to Maintain: You are responsible for
reporting maintenance issues and the upkeep
of the apartment including all appliances and
amenities. The landlord is responsible for the
repairs needed due to typical wear and tear.
• Sublet Rules: You must aquire written permission
from your landlord before subletting. Sunletting
fees are common.
• Disturbance: You are not to disturb your neighbor
with excessive noise, music, guests etc.
